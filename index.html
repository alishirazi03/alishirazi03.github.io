SARS-CoV-2 Genomic Analysis Pipeline

From Assembly to Drug Target Discovery

Comprehensive end-to-end bioinformatics pipeline analyzing real SARS-CoV-2 pandemic data. Assembled complete viral genome (29,903 bp), performed functional annotation identifying 11 major ORFs, traced evolutionary origins through phylogenetic analysis, and identified guanylate kinase as a potential antiviral drug target. Self-directed learning project demonstrating transition from wet-lab to computational biology.

Key Analyses:
  • Genome Assembly: SPAdes de novo assembly from raw Illumina reads (N50: 29,903 bp)
  • Genome Annotation: Prokka functional annotation, PCR primer design for diagnostics
  • Evolutionary Analysis: Multiple sequence alignment, natural origin confirmation
  • Phylogenetic Dating: Molecular clock analysis, tMRCA estimation (Oct-Nov 2019)
  • Drug Target Discovery: Metabolic pathway analysis, therapeutic target identification

Assessment of the role of ATP8B1 Gene in Gallstone in Pakistani Population

My final year project involved investigating the genetic variants of the ATP8B1 gene and their association with gallstone disease within the Pakistani population. By conducting a case-control study, I aimed to identify specific mutations that might predispose individuals to cholestatic liver conditions and gallstone formation. The research focused on understanding how alterations in this p-type ATPase transporter affect bile acid secretion and lipid homeostasis. This study provides a foundational genetic profile that can assist in the early diagnosis and personalized management of gallbladder disorders. The project successfully bridged the gap between clinical observation and molecular genetic validation.

Key Analyses:
  • Variant Identification: Conducted a comprehensive screening of the ATP8B1 gene to identify pathogenic and benign variants relevant to the local population.
  • Association Study: Performed statistical analysis to determine the correlation between specific genetic markers and the prevalence of gallstone disease.
  • Functional Prediction: Utilized bioinformatics tools to predict the impact of identified non-synonymous mutations on the protein's structural integrity and function.
  • Case-Control Comparison: Analyzed genetic data from patients and healthy controls to pinpoint statistically significant risk factors.
  • Population Specificity: Focused specifically on the Pakistani population to account for regional genetic diversity and unique hereditary patterns.

What bioinformatics skills did you gain from your SARS-CoV-2 project?
I gained hands-on experience with genome assembly (SPAdes, QUAST), functional annotation (Prokka), sequence alignment (BLAST, MUSCLE), phylogenetics (FastTree, LSD2), pathway analysis (BioCyc), and data visualization. What unique advantage do you bring to a research team?
My primary advantage is that I have experience in both wet-lab techniques and bioinformatics tools. While many researchers specialize in only one area, I can perform the physical DNA extraction and PCR in the lab, and then transition immediately to the computational side to handle genome assembly and clinical data analysis. This "hybrid" expertise allows me to troubleshoot experimental errors more effectively because I understand the biological context of the data I am processing.
